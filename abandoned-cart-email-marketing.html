Best Practices for Abandoned Cart Email Marketing

Written By Trey Collier

Best Practices for Abandoned Cart Email Marketing

18th September 2020

For many eCommerce businesses, seeing abandoned carts is one of the most frustrating sites to see. The customers add products to their cart, gets to the checkout, just about to pay, then they’re gone.


More than 70% of online shoppers abandon their cart at checkout, and unfortunately, this is a number that has been on the rise.

However, you can combat this phenomenon by simply implementing an abandoned cart email strategy.

One of the biggest mistakes that eCommerce businesses make, is simply allowing this person to leave, and never make an attempt to win back that customer. Much of this business can be RECOVERED, with a simple email.


The Figures


For those that are not familiar, Instagram Stories are similar to that of Snapchat Stories. They are posts (pictures or videos) which will remain on a users ‘Story’ for 24 hours. Now before you decide to ignore this idea, you should know the following:

When it comes down to those that abandon their cart at checkout, studies show that 24% of these shoppers run into some issues regarding navigation, or simply because they are not ready to buy.

Retargeting and convincing shoppers that have abandoned cart make up for around 28% of revenues for eCommerce businesses, so it is most definitely worthwhile to adopt these practices in your business.


Automation


The most successful eCommerce stores automate everything, and so should you. Whether it be acknowledging an order placed, an order being shipped, or a cart being abandoned.

Ideally, we do not want customers to abandon their cart at checkout, so one simple way of reducing the chance of this is to show the shopper a popup message when they exit the checkout, convincing them to continue with the purchase, or even asking how the issue can be resolved.

Thereafter, ensure you have an email sent out within half an hour or an hour at the most after the shopper exits the checkout. They may still be on the site, and you have a good chance of winning back the shopper.

Be sure to follow up with the first email in a week, with a sense of urgency, including a call-to-action (CTA) and maybe even an incentive such as a discount.


Abandoned Cart Recovery Strategies


Your email for abandoned cart could incorporate content that has various values:


Showcase trust and Credibility in your Emails


This is where social proof can help you convert those first-time visitors or sceptical buyers.

Product ratings and reviews are one of the best ways to do this so that those prospects can see that other people (like themselves) have happily purchased from you before.

Given 88% of consumers claim that ratings and reviews influenced their buying decision, this opens up a big opportunity for you.


Put a Single Product in the Spotlight


The chances are, that shoppers add multiple products to their basket, however, do not have equal purchasing intent for all of them. I am inviting you to think about retargeting that person with a single product, as this is also likely to trigger them to think about all the other products they had in their basket at that time.

Here are 3 tips to improve your recovery emails:

  1. Use part of your campaign to focus solely on one product, and really put it in the spotlight. The best thing to do here is to focus on your best selling product in the shopper’s basket, as it has converted with many of your other customers.

  2. Highlight some of the key selling points of that product specifically. Rather than using copy that describes your brand, and the abandoned cart, communicate the biggest reasons why the shopper should continue with the purchase.

  3. Include reviews of that spotlight product. The combination of your and your customers’ own words will give your prospective customer the full spectrum of information to aid them in their purchasing decision.

Reward Loyalty


One common way eCommerce businesses attempt to win back customers is to offer incentives through the use of coupons, and make no mistake, these can work. However, we have encoded our customers to now expect this. The risk here is the gradual devaluing of your products and does not make your loyal customers feel appreciated.

One way we can combat this is to split your email campaign between new shoppers, and returning customers. For new shoppers, a simple first time discount can be effective, but for returning customers, something a little different is required, this is how to do it:


  1. First, look at your global Average Order Value (AOV). For this example, let’s assume that is $100.

  2. Then look at your reward tiers in your loyalty program. What you want to determine is how many loyalty points a member needs to get at least a 10% discount (based on your global AOV). Let’s say 100 points equals a $10 discount.

  3. Create a segment in your email marketing software for all Rewards members that also has more than 100 points.

  4. Send that segment a different abandoned cart email campaign, where you don’t include a new discount. Instead, you highlight the fact that they have loyalty points which they could apply to this purchase to get at least 10% off.


Not only does this tactic convert incredibly well, you are not issuing a new discount, but rather you are just encouraging your loyal customers to complete their purchase with points they have built up. Rewards do increase conversions by around 40%.


Maintain Contact


Some shoppers may simply abandon their cart due to timing not being right, whether they do not have the money, or they do not need the product until a later date.

For this reason, it is best to keep that person in the loop. However, after a few emails about this, you cannot email them again without their consent, which can be a potential customer lost.

For this reason, one last attempt to recover that customer is to include one email at the end of your recovery campaign, inviting them to subscribe to your mailing list. This way, you can enter them in a new automated drip campaign, or simply send them regular newsletters about new or top-selling products.


Getting Started


Improving your abandoned cart recovery email strategy take time and is a process in which you gradually improve aspects.

Start by taking one point mentioned above, and begin implementing it this week.
